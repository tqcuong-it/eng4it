### 📖 Reading Diagnostic (20 phút)

Đọc đoạn văn ngắn (300 từ) và làm **10 câu hỏi**:

**Passage: Urban Farming Revolution**

Urban farming is transforming cities worldwide. Once considered a hobby for environmental enthusiasts, growing food in urban spaces has become a serious response to food security challenges. Rooftop gardens in New York, vertical farms in Singapore, and community allotments in London are all examples of this growing trend.

The technology driving urban farming has advanced significantly. Hydroponic systems, which grow plants without soil using nutrient-rich water, can produce crops year-round regardless of weather conditions. LED lighting optimized for plant growth enables farming in windowless warehouses and underground spaces. Some urban farms now produce 350 times more food per square meter than traditional agriculture.

Critics argue that urban farming cannot replace conventional agriculture at scale. The energy costs of indoor farming remain high, and staple crops like wheat and rice are impractical to grow in controlled environments. However, supporters counter that urban farms excel at producing fresh vegetables, herbs, and salad greens — products that lose quality during transportation and storage.

The social benefits of urban farming extend beyond food production. Community gardens have been shown to reduce crime rates, improve mental health, and strengthen social connections in neighborhoods. Educational programs in urban farms teach children about nutrition and environmental stewardship. In Detroit, urban farming has helped revitalize abandoned lots, turning urban blight into productive green spaces.

**Questions:**

**T/F/NG (3 câu):**
1. Urban farming was always taken seriously. → __________
2. Hydroponic systems require soil to function. → __________
3. Urban farms are more profitable than traditional farms. → __________

**Multiple Choice (3 câu):**
4. Urban farms can produce up to:
   - A) 100 times more food per square meter
   - B) 250 times more food per square meter
   - C) 350 times more food per square meter

5. Critics say urban farming:
   - A) is too expensive
   - B) cannot replace traditional farming at scale
   - C) produces low-quality food

6. In Detroit, urban farming has:
   - A) replaced all traditional shops
   - B) helped revitalize abandoned lots
   - C) caused property prices to drop

**Summary Completion (2 câu):**
Urban farming uses (7) __________ systems to grow plants without soil. Community gardens have been shown to reduce (8) __________ rates.

**Matching (2 câu):**
Match the city with the farming type:
9. Singapore → __________
10. London → __________

A. Vertical farms | B. Rooftop gardens | C. Community allotments

{{< spoiler "Đáp án" >}}
1. FALSE 2. FALSE 3. NOT GIVEN 4. C 5. B 6. B 7. hydroponic 8. crime 9. A 10. C
{{< /spoiler >}}

### ✍️ Writing Diagnostic (15 phút)

Viết **chỉ phần Introduction + Overview** cho Task 1 (5 phút):

**Data:** Online shopping revenue: 2015 ($300B) → 2020 ($800B) → 2025 ($1.5T projected)

Viết **chỉ 1 Body Paragraph** cho Task 2 (10 phút):

**Topic:** "Technology makes people's lives simpler." — Viết 1 paragraph **đồng ý**.

### Tự đánh giá Writing:
- [ ] Introduction có paraphrase tốt?
- [ ] Overview nêu xu hướng chính?
- [ ] Body paragraph có topic sentence?
- [ ] Có example cụ thể?
- [ ] Grammar đa dạng? Ít lỗi?

### 🎤 Speaking Diagnostic (10 phút)

Ghi âm 3 câu trả lời:

**Part 1 (30 giây):** "What do you enjoy most about your job or studies?"

**Part 2 (2 phút):** "Describe a place you would like to visit in the future."

**Part 3 (45 giây):** "How has tourism changed in the last 20 years?"

### Tự đánh giá Speaking:
- [ ] Fluency: Nói trôi chảy? Ít dừng?
- [ ] Vocabulary: Từ vựng đa dạng?
- [ ] Grammar: Dùng nhiều thì và cấu trúc?
- [ ] Pronunciation: Rõ ràng? Tự nhiên?
