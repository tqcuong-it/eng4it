## 📝 TASK 1: Bar Chart

> *The bar chart below shows the percentage of adults who participated in different leisure activities in two countries, Country A and Country B, in 2022.*

**Dữ liệu:**

| Hoạt động | Country A | Country B |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| Reading | 45% | 30% |
| Watching TV | 70% | 82% |
| Exercise/Sport | 38% | 22% |
| Social media | 55% | 68% |
| Cooking | 25% | 40% |
| Gardening | 20% | 12% |
| Gaming | 30% | 45% |

**Gợi ý Overview:**
- Watching TV phổ biến nhất ở cả 2 nước
- Country A thiên về hoạt động thể chất (exercise, gardening); Country B thiên về hoạt động digital (social media, gaming)

> *In many countries, young people are choosing to leave rural areas and move to cities. Do you think the advantages of this trend outweigh the disadvantages?*

> *Give reasons for your answer and include any relevant examples from your own knowledge or experience.*

## 📄 Bài mẫu tham khảo

### Task 1 — Bài mẫu (Band 6.5)

> The bar chart compares the proportion of adults engaging in seven leisure activities in Country A and Country B in 2022.
>
> Overall, watching television was the most popular activity in both countries. However, there were notable differences in preferences, with Country A favouring physical activities while Country B leaned towards digital entertainment.
>
> Watching TV attracted the highest participation in both nations, with 82% in Country B and 70% in Country A. Social media usage was also more prevalent in Country B (68%) compared to Country A (55%), and similarly, gaming was more popular in Country B at 45% versus 30% in Country A.
>
> Conversely, Country A showed stronger engagement in physical and outdoor activities. Exercise and sport attracted 38% of adults in Country A, compared to just 22% in Country B. Reading was also more common in Country A (45% versus 30%), as was gardening (20% versus 12%). Interestingly, cooking was one area where Country B surpassed Country A, with 40% participation compared to 25%.

**Số từ:** 160 từ ✅

### Task 2 — Bài mẫu (Band 6.5)

> In recent decades, there has been a growing trend of young people migrating from rural areas to urban centres in search of better opportunities. While this movement has some negative consequences, I believe that the advantages outweigh the disadvantages.
>
> The primary benefit of rural-to-urban migration is access to superior employment opportunities. Cities typically offer a wider range of jobs across various industries, from technology to finance, which are often unavailable in rural areas. Moreover, young people who move to cities can access better educational institutions and healthcare facilities, which contribute to their personal and professional development. For example, a young person from a rural village in Vietnam may move to Ho Chi Minh City to attend university and subsequently secure a well-paying job in the IT sector.
>
> However, this trend does carry some significant drawbacks. When large numbers of young people leave the countryside, rural communities face labour shortages, particularly in agriculture. This can lead to economic decline in these areas. Additionally, the rapid influx of young migrants into cities can worsen problems such as overcrowding, traffic congestion, and rising living costs. Young migrants themselves may also experience loneliness and the loss of cultural connections with their home communities.
>
> In conclusion, although the migration of young people to cities creates challenges for both rural and urban areas, the greater access to opportunities for education, employment, and personal growth makes this trend largely positive. Governments should implement policies to develop rural areas simultaneously to mitigate the negative effects.

**Số từ:** 228 từ (cần bổ sung thêm ~25 từ)

**Cách bổ sung:** Thêm 1 câu ví dụ vào Body 2:
> *In many developing countries, for instance, small farming villages have seen their populations decline by half over the past two decades, leaving mostly elderly residents behind.*

→ Tổng: **258 từ** ✅
