## 📖 Reading Passage: Urban Farming — Growing Food in the City

> **Paragraph A**
>
> *Urban farming, the practice of growing food within cities, has experienced remarkable growth in recent decades. What was once a niche hobby has become a global movement, with city dwellers around the world transforming rooftops, abandoned lots, and even underground spaces into productive gardens. The United Nations estimates that approximately 800 million people worldwide are now engaged in some form of urban agriculture.*

> **Paragraph B**
>
> *The motivations behind urban farming are diverse. For some, it provides access to fresh, affordable produce in areas where supermarkets are scarce — often called "food deserts." For others, growing food is a way to reduce their environmental footprint by eliminating the need for long-distance transportation. Community gardens also serve as social spaces where neighbours can connect, share knowledge, and build stronger communities.*

> **Paragraph C**
>
> *Modern urban farms employ a variety of innovative techniques. Vertical farming, which involves growing crops in stacked layers inside controlled environments, can produce up to 350 times more food per square metre than traditional farming. Hydroponics — growing plants in nutrient-rich water without soil — reduces water usage by up to 90% compared to conventional agriculture. These methods make it possible to grow food year-round, regardless of weather conditions.*

> **Paragraph D**
>
> *However, urban farming is not without its challenges. The cost of setting up indoor farms, particularly those using artificial lighting, can be prohibitively expensive. A single vertical farm can cost between $10 million and $100 million to build. Land prices in cities are typically much higher than in rural areas, making it difficult for urban farms to compete with traditional agriculture on price alone.*

> **Paragraph E**
>
> *Despite these obstacles, the future of urban farming looks promising. Cities such as Singapore, Tokyo, and Paris have incorporated urban agriculture into their official planning strategies. As technology improves and costs decrease, experts predict that urban farms will play an increasingly important role in feeding the world's growing urban population, which is expected to reach 6.7 billion by 2050.*

## ✅ Đáp án & Giải thích chi tiết

### Questions 1-5: Matching Headings

| # | Đáp án | Giải thích |
|---|--------|------------|
| 1 | **ii** — The global spread of urban farming | Đoạn A: "remarkable growth", "global movement", "800 million people worldwide" |
| 2 | **iii** — Why people choose to farm in cities | Đoạn B: "motivations behind urban farming are diverse" — liệt kê lý do |
| 3 | **v** — Advanced growing methods in cities | Đoạn C: "innovative techniques" — vertical farming, hydroponics |
| 4 | **i** — Financial barriers to urban farming | Đoạn D: "challenges", "prohibitively expensive", "$10-100 million" |
| 5 | **vi** — The outlook for city-based agriculture | Đoạn E: "future looks promising", "experts predict" |

**Headings thừa:** iv (government regulation) và vii (health risks) — không phải main idea của bất kỳ đoạn nào.

### Questions 6-10: True/False/Not Given

| # | Đáp án | Giải thích |
|---|--------|------------|
| 6 | **FALSE** | Bài nói: "in recent **decades**" (nhiều thập kỷ) → không phải chỉ thế kỷ 21. Ngoài ra, nó "once a niche hobby" → đã tồn tại từ trước |
| 7 | **TRUE** | Bài nói: "areas where supermarkets are scarce — often called 'food deserts'" ✓ |
| 8 | **TRUE** | Bài nói: "up to **350 times** more food per square metre" = "significantly more" ✓ |
| 9 | **FALSE** | Bài nói: "**reduces** water usage by up to 90%" → dùng ÍT hơn, không phải nhiều hơn |
| 10 | **TRUE** | Bài nói: "cost **between** $10 million and $100 million" → ít nhất $10 million ✓ |

### Questions 11-13: Sentence Completion

| # | Đáp án | Vị trí trong bài |

| 12 | **nutrients** | Đoạn C: "nutrient-rich water" → "rich in nutrients" |
| 13 | **6.7 billion** | Đoạn E: "expected to reach 6.7 billion by 2050" |

## 💡 Tips cho Practice Test

1. **Luôn đặt đồng hồ** — 15 phút cho Passage 1 là đủ
2. **Làm Matching Headings trước** vì cần skim toàn bài → giúp hiểu cấu trúc
3. **T/F/NG theo thứ tự** trong bài → dùng đoạn vừa đọc để locate nhanh
4. **Sentence Completion cuối** — scan keywords cụ thể
5. **Kiểm tra word limit** — "NO MORE THAN TWO WORDS" nghĩa là 1-2 từ!
