## 📝 TASK 1: Line Graph

> *The line graph below shows the average price of a house (in thousands of USD) in three cities from 2005 to 2025.*

**Dữ liệu:**

| Năm | New York | Sydney | Berlin |
|-----|----------|--------|--------|
| 2005 | 350 | 280 | 120 |
| 2010 | 300 | 320 | 130 |
| 2015 | 420 | 450 | 180 |
| 2020 | 500 | 520 | 250 |
| 2025 | 480 | 550 | 310 |

**Gợi ý nhanh:**
- Overview: Xu hướng tăng chung, Sydney vượt New York, Berlin tăng nhanh nhất
- Body 1: New York (giảm 2005-2010, rồi tăng mạnh, giảm nhẹ 2020-2025) + Sydney (tăng đều)
- Body 2: Berlin (thấp nhất nhưng tăng gấp 2.5 lần)

## 📄 Bài mẫu tham khảo

### Task 1 — Bài mẫu (Band 6.5)

> The line graph illustrates changes in average house prices, measured in thousands of US dollars, in New York, Sydney, and Berlin over a 20-year period from 2005 to 2025.
>
> Overall, all three cities experienced a general upward trend in house prices, with Sydney reaching the highest level by 2025. Notably, Berlin, despite having the lowest prices throughout, showed the most dramatic rate of increase.
>
> New York started with the highest average house price at $350,000 in 2005 but experienced a decline to $300,000 by 2010, likely due to the financial crisis. Prices then recovered and rose sharply to $500,000 in 2020, before falling slightly to $480,000 in 2025. Meanwhile, Sydney's prices grew steadily from $280,000 in 2005 to $550,000 in 2025, overtaking New York around 2010.
>
> Berlin began at a considerably lower level of $120,000 in 2005. However, the city witnessed consistent growth over the entire period, with prices more than doubling to reach $310,000 by 2025. Despite this rapid growth, Berlin remained the most affordable city of the three.

**Số từ:** 172 từ ✅

### Task 2 — Bài mẫu (Band 6.5)

> Air pollution has become one of the most pressing environmental challenges facing cities worldwide. This essay will examine the primary causes of urban air pollution and suggest measures that both governments and individuals can take to address this problem.
>
> The main cause of air pollution in cities is the high volume of vehicle emissions. As urban populations grow, the number of private cars on the roads increases significantly, releasing harmful gases such as carbon monoxide and nitrogen dioxide into the atmosphere. Another major contributor is industrial activity. Many factories located in or near urban areas emit toxic chemicals and particulate matter as by-products of their manufacturing processes. Additionally, construction projects and the burning of waste materials generate substantial amounts of dust and pollutants that further degrade air quality.
>
> To tackle this issue, governments should invest heavily in public transportation systems to encourage people to use buses and trains instead of private vehicles. Implementing stricter emission standards for factories and offering tax incentives for companies that adopt cleaner technologies would also be effective. Furthermore, governments could promote the use of electric vehicles by providing subsidies and building charging infrastructure. On an individual level, people can contribute by using public transport, cycling, or carpooling for daily commutes. Reducing energy consumption at home and supporting eco-friendly businesses are also simple but meaningful steps.
>
> In conclusion, while vehicle emissions and industrial activity are the primary causes of urban air pollution, a combination of government policies and individual actions can significantly improve air quality in cities. It is essential that both authorities and citizens work together to create a cleaner and healthier urban environment.

**Số từ:** 255 từ ✅
